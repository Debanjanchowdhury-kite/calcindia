PPF Calculator — Plan Your Tax-Free Wealth

The PPF (Public Provident Fund) is one of India's most popular long-term savings instruments, backed by the Government of India. It offers EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax status — contributions, interest earned, and maturity proceeds are all completely tax-free. The current PPF interest rate is 7.10% per annum (Q3 FY 2025-26).

This page works as a complete PPF interest calculator and PPF return calculator. Whether you contribute annually or want a PPF calculator monthly view, our tool handles both. Account holders maintaining a post office PPF account can use this same calculator since rates and rules are identical to bank PPF accounts.

PPF Calculator for HDFC, SBI & Other Banks

The PPF scheme is governed by the central government, so the interest rate and rules are exactly the same whether you open your account with HDFC Bank, SBI, ICICI Bank or your local post office. That means a PPF calculator for an HDFC account produces the same maturity figure as one for SBI or the post office — only the branch where you deposit changes. You can open just one PPF account in your name across all banks and post offices combined.

HDFC Bank account holders can open and manage a PPF account online through NetBanking, set up an auto-debit standing instruction for monthly contributions, and track the balance alongside their savings account. The same maturity result shown by this calculator applies to that HDFC-held PPF account.

PPF Tax Benefits Under Section 80C

Contributions to the PPF scheme qualify for a deduction of up to ₹1,50,000 per financial year under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The interest credited each year and the final maturity amount are both fully exempt from tax, giving the PPF its rare EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) status. Because the account runs for a base period of 15 years, the compounding combined with the Section 80C benefit makes it one of the most efficient tax-saving instruments available to Indian residents.

PPF Calculation Formula

A = P × ((1 + r)^n – 1) / r × (1 + r)

Where A = Maturity, P = Annual contribution, r = Interest rate (decimal), n = Years

PPF Key Features

  • Tenure: 15 years (extendable in blocks of 5)
  • Min Investment: ₹500 per year
  • Max Investment: ₹1,50,000 per year
  • Interest Rate: 7.10% (Q3 FY 2025-26) — compounded annually
  • Tax Benefit: 80C deduction + tax-free interest + tax-free maturity
  • Loan Facility: Available from Year 3 to Year 6
  • Partial Withdrawal: Allowed from Year 7

FD Calculator — Fixed Deposit Maturity

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a financial instrument provided by banks where you deposit a lump sum amount for a fixed tenure at a predetermined interest rate. FDs offer guaranteed returns and are considered one of the safest investment options in India.

FD Calculation Formula (Compound Interest)

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n × t)

Where A = Maturity, P = Principal, r = Annual rate, n = Compounding frequency, t = Years

PPF vs FD — Detailed Comparison

  • Lock-in: PPF 15 years | FD 7 days to 10 years
  • Returns: PPF 7.10% (govt-set) | FD 6.5% - 9% (bank-set)
  • Tax on Interest: PPF tax-free | FD taxed at slab rate
  • 80C Benefit: PPF yes | FD only on 5-year tax-saver FD
  • Liquidity: PPF restricted | FD easy premature withdrawal (penalty)
  • Risk: Both zero risk (govt & bank backed)
  • Compounding: PPF annual | FD quarterly (better)

How to Maximize Your PPF Returns

  1. Invest by 5th of Month: Interest is calculated on minimum balance between 5th and end of month
  2. Max Out Annually: Contribute ₹1.5L each year for maximum compounding
  3. Lump Sum at Year Start: Invest entire ₹1.5L in April for full year's interest
  4. Extend After 15 Years: Continue earning tax-free interest in 5-year blocks
  5. Avoid Withdrawal: Let compounding work its magic

PPF Example — ₹1.5L Annual for 15 Years

  • Interest earned: ₹18,18,209
  • Maturity value: ₹40,68,209
  • Tax saved over 15 years: ~₹6,00,000 (at 30% slab)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current PPF interest rate?
The current PPF interest rate for Q3 FY 2025-26 is 7.10% per annum, compounded annually. Rates are reviewed and announced quarterly by the Government of India.
Is PPF interest tax-free?
Yes, PPF is an EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) investment. Contributions qualify for 80C deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh, interest earned is tax-free, and maturity proceeds are completely tax-free.
Can I withdraw money from PPF before maturity?
PPF has a 15-year lock-in but partial withdrawal is allowed from Year 7. Loan facility is available from Year 3 to Year 6. Full withdrawal only at maturity or in case of emergencies after 5 years.
Is FD interest taxable?
Yes, FD interest is fully taxable at your income tax slab rate. TDS at 10% is deducted by banks if interest exceeds ₹40,000/year (₹50,000 for senior citizens). 5-year tax-saver FDs qualify for 80C deduction but interest remains taxable.
Which is better — PPF or FD?
PPF is better for long-term tax-free wealth building (15+ years). FD is better for short-to-medium term goals with flexibility (1-5 years). Combine both for a balanced portfolio.
What is the minimum and maximum PPF deposit?
Minimum ₹500 per financial year, maximum ₹1,50,000 per financial year. You can deposit in lump sum or up to 12 installments throughout the year.
