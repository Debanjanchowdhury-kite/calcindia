Introduction

Welcome to IndiCalculator (indicalculator.in), a free Indian financial calculator website. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being fully transparent about how we collect, use, and safeguard any information relating to your use of our website.

This Privacy Policy applies to all pages and services offered on IndiCalculator, including our EMI Calculator, SIP Calculator, Income Tax Calculator, GST Calculator, PPF/FD Calculator, and all associated blog articles and informational content.

By accessing or using IndiCalculator, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please discontinue your use of our website.

IndiCalculator is a purely static website hosted on GitHub Pages. We do not operate servers that process or store user data. All calculations performed on this website take place entirely within your browser and are never transmitted to any server operated by us.

In Plain English: Our calculators are 100% client-side. Whatever numbers you enter into our EMI, SIP, or Tax calculators stay in your browser only. We never see, store, or process your financial data.

This policy is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India, including the Information Technology Act, 2000, and the Information Technology (Amendment) Act, 2008. For users outside India, we comply with applicable international standards to the extent reasonably practicable.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Enter in Calculators

When you use any of our financial calculators — including the EMI Calculator, SIP Return Calculator, Income Tax Calculator (Old vs New Regime), GST Calculator, PPF Calculator, or Fixed Deposit (FD) Calculator — you may enter numerical values such as loan amounts, interest rates, tenure, income, investment amounts, and GST rates.

We do not collect, store, log, or transmit any of the following from calculator interactions:

  • Loan amounts, EMI figures, or principal values
  • Income or salary information entered into the tax calculator
  • Investment amounts or SIP values
  • GST amounts, tax rates, or business transaction figures
  • PPF or FD deposit amounts, maturity values, or interest calculations

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

Like virtually all websites, certain technical information is automatically collected when you visit IndiCalculator. This is done through third-party services (Google Analytics and Google AdSense) described in detail in Sections 2 and 3 below. The types of data automatically collected may include:

  • IP Address: Your Internet Protocol address (anonymised before storage — see Section 2)

This information is collected to help us understand how our website is being used, identify popular content, fix errors, and improve the overall user experience.

1.3 Information You Voluntarily Provide

IndiCalculator currently does not have any contact forms, account registration, newsletter subscriptions, or comment sections. Therefore, we do not collect any voluntarily submitted personal information such as your name, email address, phone number, or postal address through our website at this time.

If you contact us via email at contact@indicalculator.in or through our GitHub repository, any information you include in your message (such as your name and email address) will be used solely for the purpose of responding to your enquiry and will not be shared with third parties.

2. Google Analytics

2.1 What Google Analytics Is

IndiCalculator uses Google Analytics 4 (GA4), a web analytics service provided by Google LLC, 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA ("Google"). Google Analytics uses cookies and similar technologies to collect and analyse information about how visitors use our website.

2.2 What Google Analytics Collects

Google Analytics collects data such as:

  • Pages you visit and the sequence in which you visit them
  • Time and duration of your visits
  • The website or source that referred you to IndiCalculator
  • Approximate geographic location (country and state, not precise address)
  • Device, operating system, and browser information
  • Events such as button clicks and calculator interactions (aggregated, anonymised)
  • Session count and engagement metrics

This data is compiled into aggregated reports that show us overall patterns of usage. We see statistics like "500 people visited the EMI Calculator from India this week" — we do not see or identify individual users.

2.3 IP Anonymisation

We have configured Google Analytics to anonymise IP addresses. This means the last octet of your IP address is masked before it is stored. For example, the IP address 203.0.113.195 would be stored as 203.0.113.0. This prevents Google Analytics from being able to identify your specific device or location beyond a general geographic area.

In Google Analytics 4 (GA4), IP addresses are not logged or stored at all — they are used only to derive location data and are immediately discarded, providing stronger privacy protection than the previous version (Universal Analytics).

2.4 Google's Data Processing

The information generated by the Analytics cookie about your use of IndiCalculator is transmitted to and stored by Google on servers in the United States. Google processes this information on our behalf to evaluate your use of the website, compile reports on website activity, and provide other services relating to website activity and internet usage.

Google may also transfer this information to third parties where required by law, or where such third parties process the information on Google's behalf. Google will not associate your IP address with any other data held by Google.

2.5 Opting Out of Google Analytics

You have several options to prevent Google Analytics from tracking your browsing activity:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on: Install the free add-on available at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout for most major browsers.
  • Browser Privacy Settings: Use your browser's built-in privacy or incognito mode, which limits cookies and tracking.
  • Cookie Management: Block or delete cookies through your browser settings (see Section 5 for details).
  • Privacy-Focused Browsers: Browsers such as Brave, Firefox with Enhanced Tracking Protection, or Safari with Intelligent Tracking Prevention block many analytics scripts by default.

For more information on how Google uses data, please visit Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

5. Cookies Policy

5.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide usage information to website owners, and in some cases, to serve targeted advertising.

Cookies typically contain a website address, the duration of the cookie, and a unique number. They cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device.

5.2 Types of Cookies Used on IndiCalculator

IndiCalculator itself does not set any first-party cookies for its core functionality. Our calculators work entirely without cookies. However, the following cookies are set by third-party services we use:

  • Google Analytics Cookies (_ga, _ga_XXXXXXXXXX, _gid): These cookies are used by Google Analytics to distinguish users and sessions. The _ga cookie lasts for 2 years, _gid lasts for 24 hours, and session cookies are deleted when you close your browser. They collect anonymised data about how you use the site.
  • Google AdSense Cookies (IDE, ANID, NID, 1P_JAR): These cookies are set by Google to serve and measure advertising. The IDE cookie (DoubleClick) is used to record interactions with advertisements. These cookies may persist for up to 13 months or until manually deleted.
  • Google Fonts: When your browser loads our web fonts from Google Fonts, Google may set cookies or log your IP address as part of that request. This is a standard behaviour of browser font requests and the data is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

5.3 Managing and Disabling Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in various ways. Please note that removing or blocking cookies can impact your user experience on some websites.

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data
  • Mobile (Android/iOS): Browser settings → Privacy → Clear Browsing Data / Manage Site Data

You can also use browser extensions such as uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger, or Cookie AutoDelete to manage cookies automatically. Most modern browsers also support a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal, though its effectiveness varies by service.

5.4 Local Storage

Some of our calculators may use your browser's Local Storage to save your last-used settings (such as loan amount or interest rate) for convenience. This data is stored only on your device, is never sent to any server, and can be cleared by clearing your browser's site data at any time.

6. Data Security

6.1 Static Website Architecture

IndiCalculator is a 100% static website hosted on GitHub Pages. This architecture has significant security advantages:

  • There is no backend server, database, or server-side processing of any kind
  • All calculator logic runs entirely within your browser using JavaScript
  • We cannot receive, store, or expose user data because there is no mechanism to do so
  • There is no login system, no user accounts, and no form submissions to our servers
  • No database means no database breach risk

6.2 HTTPS Encryption

IndiCalculator is served exclusively over HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) via GitHub Pages' built-in SSL/TLS certificates. This ensures that all communication between your browser and our website is encrypted in transit, protecting against eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle attacks.

6.3 Third-Party Data Security

While we take every reasonable measure to ensure the security of our website, we cannot guarantee the security of data processed by third-party services such as Google Analytics and Google AdSense. These services maintain their own security measures and are subject to their respective privacy policies and security certifications.

Google LLC is certified under major international security frameworks including ISO/IEC 27001, SOC 2, and Privacy Shield successor frameworks, providing strong assurances about the security of data they process.

6.4 Reporting Security Issues

If you discover any security vulnerability or data-related concern with IndiCalculator, please report it responsibly by emailing us at contact@indicalculator.in or opening an issue on our GitHub repository. We are committed to addressing legitimate security concerns promptly.

SECTION 10

10. Your Rights

10.1 Rights Under Indian Law

As an Indian resident, your digital privacy rights are governed primarily by:

  • The Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act): The foundational legislation governing electronic commerce and digital privacy in India
  • The IT (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011: Specifies obligations for entities handling sensitive personal data
  • The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA): India's comprehensive personal data protection law, which establishes rights for "Data Principals" (individuals) and obligations for "Data Fiduciaries" (organisations processing data)

Under Indian privacy law, to the extent applicable, you have the right to:

  • Right to Information: Know what personal data about you is collected and how it is used
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data (where applicable)
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: Have any privacy complaints addressed promptly
  • Right to Nominate: Under DPDPA 2023, nominate someone to exercise your rights in case of incapacitation

10.2 Opting Out of Personalised Advertising

You have the explicit right to opt out of interest-based or personalised advertising. IndiCalculator respects this right and provides the following opt-out mechanisms:

  • Visit Google Ads Settings to control what data Google uses to show you ads
  • Toggle off "Personalised ads" in Google Account settings
  • Use the NAI opt-out tool at optout.networkadvertising.org
  • Enable browser-level Do Not Track (DNT) or Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals
  • Use a privacy-focused browser or extension (Brave, uBlock Origin, etc.)
Note: Opting out of personalised advertising does not prevent ads from being displayed on IndiCalculator — it simply ensures the ads you see are generic rather than tailored to your browsing history. This does not affect the functionality of our calculators in any way.

10.3 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where you have given consent to the use of cookies (e.g., by continuing to use our website with default browser settings), you may withdraw that consent at any time by clearing your cookies and adjusting your browser settings as described in Section 5.3.

10.4 Data Portability

Since IndiCalculator does not store any personal data on our servers, there is no user data held by us to port or export. All data generated by your calculator usage exists solely within your browser's local session.

For data held by Google Analytics or Google AdSense about your browsing behaviour, please refer to Google's Data Portability and Takeout tools available at myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy.

10.5 Non-Discrimination

IndiCalculator does not discriminate against users who exercise their privacy rights. Opting out of personalised ads, disabling cookies, or contacting us with privacy concerns will not affect your ability to access or use any of our calculators, blog content, or any other feature of our website. All tools remain free and fully functional regardless of your privacy choices.
